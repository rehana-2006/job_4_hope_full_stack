Understanding Child Labour

Child labour refers to work that deprives children of their childhood, potential, and dignity, and is harmful to their physical and mental development.

âš ï¸ What Constitutes Child Labour?

Work that is mentally, physically, socially or morally dangerous and harmful to children:

  • Work that interferes with their schooling
  • Work in hazardous conditions or with dangerous tools
  • Long hours that affect their physical and mental development
  • Work that exploits children economically

Indian laws protecting children from labour:

  • Right to Education Act, 2009
  • Juvenile Justice Act, 2015
  • Constitutional Article 24 (prohibition of child labour)
